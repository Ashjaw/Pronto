Komatsu Arizona Proving Grounds
HD605-10 demo loaded by a WA700-8 wheel loader.

Komatsu was the first OEM to formally partner with Pronto to bring AHS to the quarry market. Komatsu Smart Quarry Autonomous, powered by Pronto, integrates Pronto's vision-first autonomy directly into quarry-sized Komatsu haul trucks and ties the fleet into Komatsu's Smart Quarry Site fleet-management suite — so the same dealer that services the iron also services the autonomy.

The Lake Bridgeport, Texas production fleet pairs Komatsu HD605-8 and next-generation HD605-10 rigid-frame trucks with Caterpillar 775Gs on a single Pronto AHS — the world's first fully autonomous mixed-OEM quarry fleet in commercial production.

Bell brought articulated dump trucks onto the Pronto AHS roster. Where Komatsu and Caterpillar cover the rigid-frame quarry and mining classes, Bell extends the platform to articulated haul — the right tool for variable terrain, soft ground, and tight site geometries that a rigid-frame truck cannot work.

Bell Trucks America's exclusive autonomy agreement with Pronto means every Bell ADT delivered into the U.S. and Mexico can be Pronto-equipped from new, or retrofitted post-delivery, through the same dealer network that services the trucks today.

04 / Reference Deployment

Lake Bridgeport, TX. The mixed-fleet proof point.

Over an eight-month production run, Heidelberg Materials' Lake Bridgeport quarry autonomously hauled more than two million tons of limestone — using a single Pronto AHS to coordinate Caterpillar 775Gs alongside Komatsu HD605-8 and HD605-10 rigid-frame trucks.

Operator
Heidelberg Materials — one of the world's largest integrated manufacturers of building materials.
OEMs
Caterpillar 775G + Komatsu HD605-8 / HD605-10, on one autonomy stack.
Result
2M+ tons moved autonomously in under 8 months — equivalent to ~20,000 freight cars of limestone.
Next
Rolling out across Mitchell (Indiana) and Servtex (Texas) in 2026; 100+ trucks globally over the next three years.
