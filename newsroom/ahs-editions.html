Pronto Unveils AHS Editions: The Industry's First Tiered Autonomous Haulage Portfolio

Vision, VLR, and VLR 360 — three product lines designed around the physics and economics of every mining environment.

Pronto has announced the industry's first tiered autonomous haulage system portfolio. The three-edition lineup matches sensor philosophy and safety architecture to the physics and economics of each deployment environment — from 30-tonne articulated dump trucks in aggregate quarries to 400-tonne ultra-class haulers in deep open-pit copper and iron ore mines.

Three Editions

Pronto AHS Vision uses camera arrays and Pronto's proprietary AI platform. With no LiDAR and no requirement for pre-mapped routes, Vision has the lowest barrier to entry of any production-grade AHS on the market. It is designed for rapid deployment at quarry and aggregate operations where site geometry changes frequently and infrastructure investment must be minimal.

Pronto AHS VLR adds long-range LiDAR and radar to the Vision camera stack. The system is fail-operational by design — if any single sensor modality degrades, the system maintains autonomous production rather than entering a safe-stop. VLR is built for operations where dust, fog, or precipitation would otherwise interrupt autonomous cycles.

Pronto AHS VLR 360 extends the sensor array to full 360-degree coverage. Designed for ultra-class haulers operating in high-density, complex pits — where vehicles from multiple OEMs, light vehicles, and personnel may share a haul corridor — VLR 360 provides situational awareness that cannot be achieved with forward-facing or limited-angle arrays.

"VLR 360 isn't a luxury spec — for ultra-class operations at night, in dust, with mixed traffic, it's the minimum viable safety system."

All three editions are OEM-agnostic across the full Pronto supported range, including the Komatsu 980E and Caterpillar 798. First VLR 360 production deployments are currently underway, with customer announcements to follow in 2026.
