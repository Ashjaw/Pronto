Pronto AHS Hauls Over 2 Million Tonnes at Heidelberg Materials' Lake Bridgeport

North America's first fully autonomous mixed-fleet quarry hit the 2-million-tonne mark in under eight months — with zero autonomy-related incidents.

Pronto AHS has surpassed 2 million tonnes of limestone hauled at Heidelberg Materials' Lake Bridgeport quarry in Texas — a milestone reached in under eight months of autonomous production operations. The site runs a mixed fleet of Komatsu HD605 and Caterpillar 775G rigid haul trucks, making it North America's first fully autonomous mixed-fleet quarry in commercial production.

The system runs on vision only — camera arrays and proprietary AI, with no LiDAR and no pre-mapped routes. Rather than relying on a static map of the quarry bench, the Pronto AHS vision stack navigates dynamic environments in real time, including truck congestion, blast events, weather changes, and shifting haul road geometry. The approach eliminates the site-mapping overhead that slows traditional AHS deployments.

"Two million tonnes on a mixed fleet, zero autonomy-related incidents — that's the number that matters. We deployed this to see if it could hold a production rhythm. It can."

Heidelberg Materials has contracted 100+ autonomous trucks across 12+ sites globally, with Lake Bridgeport serving as the primary integration and training site ahead of international deployments. The milestone validates the production scalability of the Pronto platform and underpins the broader multi-site expansion announced in April 2026.
