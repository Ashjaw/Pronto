Mariana Minerals Taps Pronto to Automate Utah's Copper One

The world's first autonomous-first mine deploys Pronto AHS Vision integrated with MineOS for end-to-end driverless haulage.

Mariana Minerals and Pronto have begun deployment at Copper One in Utah — the world's first mine designed to be autonomous from the outset. Unlike retrofits of legacy operations, Copper One was acquired and configured with full-fleet autonomy as the foundational operating model. Three haul trucks have entered service in the initial phase, with full fleet expansion scheduled through 2027.

Pronto AHS Vision is integrated directly with MineOS, Mariana's proprietary mine operating system, enabling fully driverless dispatch with no human in the control loop. The integration handles end-to-end haulage cycle management — dispatch, routing, load assignment, and dump — without requiring manual override capability as a standard operating mode. The target production rate is 50,000 tonnes of copper cathode per year by 2030, representing a 20-fold increase over current output.

"We're not retrofitting autonomy onto a legacy mine. We bought Copper One because we could build the world's only autonomous-first mine. Pronto's system is the foundation."

Copper One is Pronto's first major partnership announced since the company's acquisition by Atoms Inc., led by Travis Kalanick. The MineOS-to-AHS integration architecture developed at Copper One is expected to be licensed to other operations, creating a replicable model for autonomous-first mine design.
