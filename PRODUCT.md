# Pronto — Approved Manufacturers

## Product Purpose
Landing page for Pronto.ai's Autonomous Haulage System (AHS). Communicates the approved OEM manufacturer list — Komatsu, Caterpillar, Bell Equipment — and the models validated for Pronto AHS retrofit. Primary goal: establish technical credibility and OEM-agnostic differentiation for quarry/mine operators considering autonomous haulage.

## Register
brand

## Users
Mine and quarry operations managers, fleet directors, and procurement leads at large aggregates and mining companies. Decision-makers evaluating a multi-million-dollar technology retrofit. They are data-literate, skeptical of hype, and respond to specificity — model numbers, tonnage, site names, production figures.

## Brand Voice
Precise. Industrial. Confident without bravado. Speaks to operators, not investors. No metaphors, no startup-speak. The aesthetic should feel like a technical spec sheet elevated to editorial quality — a mining trade publication's best cover story, not a SaaS landing page.

## Tone Anti-references
- SaaS hero-metrics template (big number, gradient accent, enterprise stock photos)
- AI startup purple-and-white rounded-corner look
- LinkedIn B2B corporate blue

## Brand Colors
- Black: #0F0F10 (near-black, the primary surface)
- White: #FFFFFF
- Yellow accent: #FECE3D (used sparingly — numbers, pills, em highlights)

## Typography
- PP Neue Montreal: headlines and display (weight 400–700)
- PP Fraktion Sans: body and copy (weight 300–700)
- PP Fraktion Mono: labels, badges, metadata (weight 700, uppercase, tracked)

## Strategic Principles
- OEM-agnostic is the core differentiator — one autonomy stack, any truck you own
- Specificity signals trust: real deployment names, real tonnage figures, real model numbers
- The truck silhouettes (inline SVG) are the primary visual language — industrial diagrams, not illustrations
- No external images currently available; SVG silhouettes carry the visual weight
